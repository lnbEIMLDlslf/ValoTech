Why Corporate Leaders Trust ValoLab

Regulated-industry buyers ask one question first: how do we defend this to the auditor? ValoLab's answer is structural — every action is mechanically governed, logged, and reviewable. The seven capabilities below are what makes that possible.

1

Mechanical Governance, Not Guidelines

Every AI company says their agents "follow rules." The real question is: what happens when they don't?

ValoLab enforces rules with infrastructure code that runs outside the agent — intercepting every action before and after execution. The agent doesn't have a choice. Like a building where restricted doors physically won't open without the right badge.

  • Layer 1 — Hard deny: Dangerous actions (delete files, force-push, install dependencies, modify sensitive configs) are blocked before they happen. No override without human approval.
  • Layer 2 — State machine: Every task is risk-classified (Tier 1/2/3) before work begins. High-risk actions require human approval. The system classifies independently from the agent — neither can downgrade the other.
  • Layer 3 — Audit log: Every action logged with who, when, what tier. Automated compliance review every 20 actions. Circuit breaker halts work if violation rate spikes.
  • Layer 4 — Self-reflection: Agents check their own output against all active rules before presenting it as complete.
  • Layer 5 — Independent auditor: A separate process reviews every response for rule violations, false claims, scope drift, and tier misclassification.
2

Institutional Memory That Compounds

Knowledge captured once is available everywhere, forever — typed, timestamped, and trust-labeled.

  • Structured knowledge pool: 6 memory categories — people profiles, feedback corrections, decisions with rationale, reusable patterns, evaluated research, and prioritized backlog.
  • Cross-project compounding: A lesson learned on one engagement is filed to a shared pool and available to the next. New agents inherit the full organizational knowledge on day one.
  • Freshness control: Every piece of knowledge has an expiration date. Stale information is flagged before anyone acts on it — regulations revalidated every 6 months, market data every 3 months.
  • Domain-calibrated trust: Each industry defines its own evidence standards. A nutrition claim and a legal claim are evaluated by different rules automatically.
3

Information Quality Engine

Every external claim is evaluated before it reaches you. Not "AI research" — a structured evaluation framework with academic rigor.

  • 3-axis source evaluation: Every source is assessed on method of production, independence from bias, and verifiability.
  • GRADE downgrade assessment: Evidence quality is scored for risk of bias, inconsistency, indirectness, imprecision, and publication bias.
  • Training data is banned: Every data point must trace to a live, verifiable source from the current session. No "the AI recalled a number" — if there's no live source, it says so.
  • Per-finding confidence labels: Every finding is labeled Verified, Indicated, or Suggested. No speculation as fact. No "must do" language unless the source is confirmed.
4

5

Pluggable Domain Knowledge

Industry expertise is modular. The same organization structure works for fund management, banking, food, or legal — just plug in the right domain layer.

  • Domain separation: Industry rules, regulations, ontology, and market context are stored separately from the org structure. Switch domains without rebuilding anything.
  • Source authority profiles: Each domain defines what evidence types exist and how to weight them. A regulatory claim is evaluated differently from a market-data claim.
  • Regulatory override: When law and evidence disagree, the system tracks both. Legal requirements are never overridden by "the research says otherwise."
6

3-Layer Architecture for Instant Reuse

The entire organization — departments, quality gates, accumulated knowledge — transfers to every new engagement automatically.

Layer 1 — Org
Departments, roles, playbooks. Generic. Works for any client.
Layer 2 — Domain
Industry expertise. Shared across clients in the same industry.
Layer 3 — Client
Client-specific configs. Only this changes per engagement.
  • Starting a new engagement means adding Layer 3 only. Layers 1 and 2 are inherited.
  • No rebuilding processes from scratch. Every prior engagement makes the next one smarter.
7

Full Audit Trail on Every Action

Complete traceability of who did what, when, and under what authority. Built for organizations that need to show their work.

  • Every tool call logged: Action type, file affected, tier classification, timestamp, and session ID.
  • Compliance checkpoints: Every 20 actions, an automated compliance review against all active rules.
  • Circuit breaker: If violation thresholds are exceeded, the system halts work and escalates to the human operator.
  • No silent failures: When something is blocked or flagged, the human sees why and decides what to do next.
